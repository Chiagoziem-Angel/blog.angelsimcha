On Giving Up

God didn't bring you this far just to leave you.

April 25th

I was listening to a really timeless song — Can't Give Up, by Mary Mary. I can remember listening to it as far back as 2013, over a decade ago. It's still a wonderful song. It still gets me.

Back in 2013, most of the lyrics didn't really make sense to me because I wasn't really listening to the words. All I was listening to was the energy of it — having childish vibes and all of that. But listening to it now as an adult, really paying attention to the lyrics, I understand a whole lot, and it has given me so much encouragement.

I just felt like sharing. Here's the song — listen to it yourself and see the full lyrics: Mary Mary — Can't Give Up.

Basically, the song is about two people who can't give up because they've come too far. And when you really meditate on the lyrics, you understand that God didn't bring you this far just to leave you.

I just can't give up now
Come too far from where I started from
Nobody told me the road would be easy
And I don't believe He's brought me this far to leave me.

God didn't allow you to be born, allow you to grow up, allow you to live for eighteen years or more, only for nothing — only to leave you stranded at this point in time.

It may look that way. It may look like there is no hope. It may look like there is nothing to your life at the moment. You may look back and wonder what you've been doing with your life. You may look forward and wonder what's in it for you — why am I still here? But the mere fact that you are still alive, still here, is proof that God hasn't left you and he still has so much more in store for you.

There will be mountains that I will have to climb
And there will be battles that I will have to fight
But victory or defeat, it's up to me to decide
But how can I expect to win if I never try…

One thing I want to bring out from this — we are co-creators with God in this life. We are in partnership with God. God is not a dictator. He's not someone that is going to say, okay, do this, and then force you into it. He needs your partnership. He needs your signature in this document of life. He needs you to accept the terms and conditions and be willing to go all in.

You are not a puppet he is going to string along. He's not going to take your leg or your hand and make you walk steps or take action. He will show you the way. He will show you His good plans. He will show you the direction. But it's up to you to move. And you will never know how far you can go unless you make that first step — unless you try.

This life is not an easy life. Nobody told you it would be easy. Even those you look at and think they have it all easy, don't have it all easy. We all have our advantages, but nobody has it easy at all. And you can't expect that now that you're a believer, things are just going to be smooth. Nobody ever said that.

What is mentioned in the Bible is that you will have trials and temptations — but do not be afraid, for he has already overcome. There is nothing you will pass through that you don't have the strength to overcome, that a way will not be made for you. It's all about going in with faith. Life is a life of faith. You will never know until you try.

So if you're still here, pondering the past or uncertain about the future, wondering why you're here — I believe God has already placed in you ideas, thoughts, and things to do. It's up to you to reflect, go inward, and see what is firing your heart.

What are the ideas, the plans, the purpose that are inside of you? Bring them out and begin to work with them. What are the skills, the talents, the things you've seen yourself good at? How can you bring them out, capitalize on them, maximize them, bring them into this real world? That is the essence of life.

Everybody has their own battles. Everybody has their own fight. It's just that some people persevere more than others. Some people push through. Some people don't give up.

And we have a bigger advantage — we have God on our side. He is giving us strength, but we don't maximize that advantage. We think the advantage means that when we pray, everything becomes magic. No. God is a principled God, and he blesses those who are already following his principles.

All I'm saying is: move forward. Take a leap of faith. Take that step, walk that walk, take that move. You will never know until you try. What's the worst that could happen — it doesn't work out. What's the best that could happen — it actually works out and becomes the foundation of the life you've been looking forward to having.

The choice is yours. Defeat or victory, the choice is yours. The fact that you haven't even made a choice means you've already defaulted to defeat. But you can still rise up and say no. I'm not going to give up. I'm not going to be defeated until I try.

That's just the word I felt like sharing.

Shallom 🤍
